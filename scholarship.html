Scholarships

  • Ascend Educational Fund (AEF)
    • Scholarships ranging from $2,500 to $20,000 to immigrant students and children of immigrants who are graduating from a New York City high school to attend public or private colleges and universities.
    http://ascendfundny.org/scholarship/
  • Alice T Schafer Mathematics Prize
    • Awarded to an undergraduate woman for excellence in mathematics. The nominee may be at any level in her undergraduate career but must be an undergraduate when nominated.
    https://sites.google.com/site/awmmath/programs/schafer-prize

  • JP Morgan Chase & CO.
    • The Thomas G. Labrecque Smart Start Program provides a work experience at the company as well as a full, four-year scholarship to graduating high school seniors from New York.
    https://careers.jpmorgan.com/careers/programs/smart-start
  • Posse Foundation
    • There is a three step process with this program. First, students are placed in scenerios to test their "public speaking, listening, negotiation and communication skills". As students are selected, students move to the next step, will be invited back for an interview. Lastly, the applicants will undergo a finalist workshop.
    http://www.possefoundation.org/about-posse/program-components/recruitment/nomination-process
  • Ronald McDonald House Charities
    • Scholarship provides resources that is essential to persue an area of study regarless of finacial circumstance. This is only eligible for Hispanics.
    https://www.mcdonalds.com/us/en-us/community/hacer.html
  • Society of Women Engineers
    • The scholarship supports women studying in a bachelor or graduate programs persuing careers in engineering, engineering technology and computer science.
    http://societyofwomenengineers.swe.org/scholarships
  • QuestBridge
    • If you match with one of QuestBridge's partner colleges then you will be granted with a full, four-year scholarship worth over $200,000 each. The financial package will guarantee no student loans.
    https://www.questbridge.org/high-school-students/national-college-match/scholarship-details

Remember to fill out the FAFSA which will tell you the type of financial aid you will be recieving from each school you apply to.
